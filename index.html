Fix the facinorous food

The Swedish school lunch system are failing to feed 20% of the students every day, with over 40% of the students disliking or hating the food according to SVT, Sweden’s government founded mass media company. This leads to students not getting enough energy and nutritions to concentrate in class. Having tired students does not only affect a few people, it might affect the whole class. Tired students tend to be disturbing others when they’re unable to concentrate, which lowers the educational quality for the whole class rather than a few people.

What we have to do is to pressure the schools to follow the already set standards for the school lunches, meaning that it should be good tasting, healthy, environmentally friendly and safe to eat.

We need to communicate more! The most important part is to let the students be the ones to decide if the food tastes good. There should be atleast one poll every semester to evaluate the food, ranking the best and worst dishes for the chefs to evaluate their menu and remove the disliked dishes, and continue serving the appreciated dishes.

About us

We are a group of 4 passionate students who strives to pressure the Swedish schools to enforce a better food standard for the school lunches. We are based in Umeå, Sweden and are founders of the “Make the school lunch edible throughout Sweden” campaign. Our journey started after we countless times got served uncooked rice and potatoes, way overcooked rice (porridge like), frozen pancakes and frozen hashbrowns for lunch. Since then, we have launched this campaign hoping to help improve the lunches in the schools all around Sweden by inspiring students to take their stand and communicate more with the chefs about the food.

FAQ

How can I join the movement?

Share, share, share! Tell your friends about the campaign, get engaged and be a part of our awesome community.


How can I help improving the food in my school?

Communication! When you are in school, talk to the student council about ideas for improvements that they can pass on to the higher ups.


What is your source of income as an organisation?

We are fully non profit, meaning that we do not make any money at all. This is a project that we are running in our free time to help improve the school lunches.
